Here are 5 extension activities for students who have demonstrated mastery of the essential standard "EXAMINE key concepts and influences of major belief systems on societies." in a 6th grade Social Studies class:

  1. Create a video: Ask students to create a video that showcases the impact of one major belief system on a particular society. Students can use digital resources like a computer or tablet to create the video. They can incorporate visual aids, animations, and text overlays to make their video engaging and informative.
  2. Virtual museum exhibit: Challenge students to create a virtual museum exhibit on a major belief system. They can use a variety of digital resources such as videos, images, and interactive displays to create an immersive experience. They can use 3D printing to create models of artifacts related to the belief system.
  3. Debate: Divide students into groups and have them debate the merits and drawbacks of different belief systems on societies. This activity will encourage higher-order thinking and critical analysis of the topic. Students can use digital resources like online research materials, digital debate tools, and video recording to prepare and present their arguments.
  4. Historical fiction writing: Challenge students to write a short story that illustrates the impact of a major belief system on a society. This activity will encourage students to apply their knowledge creatively and develop their storytelling skills. Students can use digital resources like writing software and online research materials to support their work.
  5. Digital map: Ask students to create a digital map that shows the spread of a major belief system across different regions and cultures. They can use online tools like Google Maps, interactive timeline tools, and digital storytelling platforms to create an engaging and informative map. Students can incorporate images, videos, and text overlays to make their map interactive and visually appealing.
